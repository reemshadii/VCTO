Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using the VCTO platform.

General Information

What is VCTO?
VCTO (Virtual Clothes Try-On) is an AI-powered tool that allows you to see how clothing will look on your specific body type. By uploading a photo of yourself and a photo of a clothing item, our system digitally maps the fabric onto your body.
Is the service free to use?
Yes! The core simulator is completely free. You can generate outfits, view color analysis, and download your results without paying. We may introduce premium features for high-definition rendering in the future.
Do I need an account to use it?
No. You can use the simulator as a "Guest" immediately. However, creating an account allows you to save your generated outfits to a dashboard, track your history, and manage your wardrobe.

Technical Support & Usage

What kind of photo should I upload?
For the best results:
  • Use a full-body or half-body shot.
  • Ensure you are facing forward.
  • Wear tight-fitting clothes (like gym wear) so the AI can accurately detect your body shape.
  • Avoid busy backgrounds and poor lighting.
Why does the clothing look distorted?
Distortion usually happens if the uploaded clothing image is wrinkled, folded, or taken at an extreme angle. Try to use flat-lay images (clothing laid flat on a surface) or clean product photos from a store website.
How accurate are the body measurements?
Our AI estimation is generally accurate within 1-2 inches. However, for precise fitting, we highly recommend using the Manual Entry option in Step 2 to input your exact measurements.
What file formats are supported?
We currently support JPG, PNG, and WEBP formats. The maximum file size per upload is 10MB.

Privacy & Security

Is my photo private?
Absolutely. If you use Guest Mode, your images are processed in real-time and deleted from our servers shortly after generation. If you have an account, images are stored securely in your private gallery until you choose to delete them.
Do you sell user data?
Never. Your biometric data (body measurements) and photos are used strictly for the purpose of generating the virtual try-on simulation. We do not share or sell this data to third-party advertisers.
